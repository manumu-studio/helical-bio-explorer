# Questions for the friend

> **Context:** This is the question list Manu sent his PhD neuroscientist friend ahead of their phone call on 2026-04-10, together with `FRIEND-BRIEF.md`. The goal was to ground the project's scientific framing in a working scientist's perspective rather than in marketing material.

## Question zero — the most important one

0. When I describe Helical as *"what you did in your thesis, but on cells instead of brains,"* does the analogy actually hold up, or am I oversimplifying? What does that comparison miss, and what does it get wrong? I want to understand the parallel properly before I build anything on top of it.

## On data and scientific relevance

1. In your thesis, did you ever work directly with **single-cell RNA-seq** data (AnnData format, `.h5ad` files, scanpy)? If so, what did you do with it?

2. If someone handed you a public single-cell dataset — say, from **CELLxGENE** — and asked you to explore it, what are the **natural questions** a scientist starts asking? (Cell-type annotation, trajectories, markers, condition-vs-condition comparisons, and so on.)

3. Is there any **public dataset** you'd recommend as a "classic" or a "hello-world" example for demonstrating a new tool? Something small, clean, and with a real story behind it.

4. In neuroscience specifically: is there a brain or neuronal dataset that is famous enough to work well as a demo? (Allen Brain Atlas, Alzheimer datasets, anything of that kind.)

## On the value of the project

5. If I showed you a web app where you could click on a cell and see "similar cells" based on embeddings from a bio foundation model, would that feel useful to you? What would it need to actually be valuable in the context of your work?

6. Which **web tools** did you rely on during your PhD to explore biological data? (CELLxGENE Explorer, UCSC Cell Browser, cBioPortal, etc.) What did you like about them, and what frustrated you?

7. Do you know of a concrete case where **AI applied to single-cell data** changed something real, either in research or in the clinic? A specific story would be incredibly useful.

## On the passion for biotech

8. When you decided to do a PhD in neuroscience, what was the thing that hooked you in the first place? Is there a biological question that still fascinates you today?

9. For someone from outside the biology world (me), what is **the single most mind-blowing idea** in modern biology that you could explain in five minutes? Something that would make an outsider stop and think *this actually matters*.

10. What do you think of the "in-silico drug discovery" framing? Is it marketing, or is it really changing the game?

## Format of your answer

Don't worry about giving me long written answers — voice notes, bullets, links, whatever is fastest for you. If any of these questions seem naive, push back and tell me so. Whatever you send, I'll use to make decisions.

Thanks. I owe you at least one beer.
